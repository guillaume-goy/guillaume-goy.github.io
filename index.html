Guillaume GOY

Post-Doctoral Researcher at XLIM
University of Limoges, France.

I am a post-doctoral researcher at the Research Center XLIM (UMR CNRS 7252) at the University of Limoges. My main subjects of interest are post-quantum Code-based cryptography and Side-Channel Attacks. I am particulary focused on Hamming Quasi-Cyclic (HQC), which was recently selected by the US National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) as the new post-quantum key exchange standard.

Previously, I was a PhD Student in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science at CEA Grenoble, France and at the Research Center XLIM (UMR CNRS 7252) at the University of Limoges. My advisors were Professor Philippe Gaborit and Antoine Loiseau.
